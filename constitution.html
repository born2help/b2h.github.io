The B2H Constitution

B2H exists to serve humanity. This purpose is permanent and cannot be replaced, diluted, or ignored.

The poor, the vulnerable, and the forgotten are not beneficiaries — they are the reason this project exists.

No person owns B2H. Leadership is a duty of service, not a right to profit or control.

All systems, funds, and authority must remain aligned with the mission to reduce human suffering and protect dignity.

Decisions must be guided by conscience, not greed; by compassion, not convenience; by responsibility, not power.

If leadership ever fails this mission, the mission must outlive leadership. The purpose of B2H must never be sacrificed for personal gain or external pressure.

Transparency is not optional. Accountability is not conditional. Trust must be earned continuously.

This constitution stands as a promise — to humanity today and to generations tomorrow — that B2H will always choose service over self.

Founder Safeguard & Ethical Compensation

B2H recognizes that leadership requires sacrifice, responsibility, and lifelong commitment.

To ensure stability, continuity, and the ability to serve humanity effectively, the Founder is entitled to ethical compensation strictly for operational leadership, global representation, family responsibility, and personal sustainability.

Founder compensation shall never be derived directly from funds allocated for humanitarian aid.

A defined portion of the Project Treasury may be allocated for: leadership expenses, travel for mission-related work, administrative operations, and basic founder livelihood — without excess, luxury, or exploitation.

The Founder does not own B2H. The Founder serves B2H.

All founder-related allocations must remain: transparent, publicly declared, and aligned with the mission.

If any future leader violates this trust, the community and the constitution retain moral authority to protect the mission.

This safeguard exists not to enrich individuals, but to ensure B2H never collapses due to neglect of its human leadership.
