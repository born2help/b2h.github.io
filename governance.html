Governance & Founder Safeguard

B2H is governed by responsibility, not ownership. Leadership exists only as a trust — never as a right to exploit.

The founder does not own B2H. The founder serves B2H.

All authority exists solely to protect the mission: helping human beings in need with dignity and sincerity.

Founder Safeguard

The founder may manage funds only for purposes directly related to humanitarian aid, operational sustainability, outreach, and the minimum requirements necessary to carry this responsibility.

No funds may be used for luxury, excess, or personal enrichment. Any expenses must be reasonable, documented, and aligned with the mission.

The founder may travel, communicate, and represent the project only to strengthen its ability to help people — never to benefit personally.

Mission Lock

The mission of B2H is permanent. It cannot be altered, diluted, or redirected by profit, pressure, popularity, or power.

No individual — including the founder — may change the purpose of this project.

If leadership ever changes, the mission must remain untouched: humanity always comes first.

Transparency Commitment

Transparency is a duty, not a feature. As B2H grows, summaries of humanitarian impact, operational use, and governance practices will be shared openly.

Trust is sacred. Without trust, this project has no right to exist.

Governance Capture Resistance

B2H is designed with the assumption that power accumulation attempts will occur over time. This framework exists to prevent silent takeovers, ethical erosion, and mission drift.

Ethics Override Governance

No governance mechanism, vote, committee, or operational decision may override the ethical mission of B2H.

If a decision is profitable, popular, or legally permissible but violates conscience, it is considered invalid.

No Capital-Based Authority

Financial participation does not grant voting rights, governance authority, or influence over mission direction.

B2H explicitly rejects governance based on capital, tokens, or investment size.

Founder Ethical Veto

The founder holds a limited ethical veto strictly to block mission distortion, exploitation, or governance capture.

This veto cannot be used for personal benefit, financial extraction, or operational micromanagement.

Multi-Layer Governance Lock

  • Public Constitution defining purpose
  • Non-negotiable Ethical Clauses
  • Private Continuity Designation

No single layer is sufficient on its own. All must align for governance legitimacy.

Protection Against Internal Drift

Committees, councils, or leadership bodies may advise and execute — but never redefine mission.

Authority is conditional. Purpose is permanent.
