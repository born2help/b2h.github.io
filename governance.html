Governance Principles

B2H is governed by responsibility, not ownership. Leadership exists only as a trust ‚Äî never as a right to exploit.

The founder serves B2H and the mission. Authority exists solely to protect the purpose: helping humanity with dignity.

Founder Safeguard

The founder manages resources only for humanitarian aid, operational sustainability, outreach, and minimum necessary requirements to carry this responsibility.

No funds may be used for luxury or personal enrichment. All actions must be documented, reasonable, and aligned with the mission.

Travel, representation, and communication are permitted only to strengthen the project.

Mission Lock

The mission of B2H is permanent. It cannot be altered, diluted, or redirected by profit, pressure, popularity, or power.

No individual ‚Äî including the founder ‚Äî may change the purpose of this project. Leadership changes must never compromise the mission.

Transparency Commitment

Transparency is a duty. Summaries of humanitarian impact, operations, and governance practices will be shared responsibly.

Trust is sacred. Without trust, the project has no right to exist.

Governance Capture Resistance

B2H assumes power accumulation attempts will occur. The framework exists to prevent silent takeovers, ethical erosion, and mission drift.

Ethics Override Governance

No governance mechanism, vote, committee, or operational decision may override the ethical mission of B2H.

Profitable, popular, or legally permissible decisions violating conscience are invalid.

No Capital-Based Authority

Financial participation does not grant governance rights or influence over mission.

B2H rejects governance based on capital, tokens, or investment size.

Founder Ethical Veto

The founder holds a limited ethical veto to block mission distortion, exploitation, or capture.

This veto cannot be used for personal benefit or operational micromanagement.

Multi-Layer Governance Lock

  • Public Constitution defining purpose
  • Non-negotiable Ethical Clauses
  • Private Continuity Designation

No single layer is sufficient alone. All must align for governance legitimacy.

Protection Against Internal Drift

Committees or leadership bodies may advise and execute but never redefine mission. Authority is conditional; purpose is permanent.
